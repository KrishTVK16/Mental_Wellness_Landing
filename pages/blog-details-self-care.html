Self Care

A self-care routine that lasts

Dr. Sarah West
• Jan 12, 2026 • 5 min read

Self-care is a buzzword that is often tossed around, but true self-care isn't just about bubble baths and face masks. It's about creating sustainable habits that support your mental, emotional, and physical well-being.

1. Identify Your Needs

Ask yourself: What do I need right now? Do I need rest, connection, movement, or solitude? Self-care looks different for everyone, and it can change from day to day.

2. Start Small

Consistency is key. Instead of overhauling your entire life, focus on small, manageable changes. Drink a glass of water first thing in the morning, take a 5-minute walk, or read a few pages of a book.

3. Set Boundaries

Protecting your energy is a vital form of self-care. Learn to say no to commitments that drain you and prioritize activities that nourish you.

"Self-care is not selfish. You cannot serve from an empty vessel." - Eleanor Brown

By prioritizing your well-being, you can show up more fully for yourself and others. Remember, self-care is a journey, not a destination.
